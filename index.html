Andreas Mastrosavvas

I am a PhD student in Social Sciences at Cardiff University, jointly supervised at the Social Data Science Lab and the School of Geography and Planning. My thesis is funded by the Economic and Social Research Council, and examines how local economic shocks and policies affect voting in distant, socially connected areas. More broadly, I am a quantitative social scientist interested in the causes and effects of socio-spatial networks and inequalities. My research draws on spatial statistics, causal inference, and new forms of data to address empirical questions on these topics.
