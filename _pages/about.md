I am a PhD candidate in Social Sciences at [Cardiff University](https://www.cardiff.ac.uk), jointly supervised at the Social Data Science Lab and the School of Geography and Planning. My thesis is funded by the [Economic and Social Research Council](https://www.ukri.org/councils/esrc/), and examines the relationship between the geographic structure of social networks and electoral outcomes using aggregate social media data and large-scale administrative records. Prior to my doctoral studies, I received postgraduate training (MSc) in Data Science and Analytics, and held posts as a research officer at the UK Office for National Statistics and the Welsh Government.

More broadly, I am a quantitative social scientist with research interests in **neighbourhood effects**, **public policy**, and **elections**. My research draws on statistical and econometric methods, spatial analysis, and new forms of data to address empirical questions on these topics, with a focus on:
- The impacts of localised interventions and socio-economic shocks
- The formation of preferences within social groups
- The spatial dimensions of social processes
