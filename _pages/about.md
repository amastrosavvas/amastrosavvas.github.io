I am a quantitative social scientist with research interests in **neighbourhood effects**, **public policy**, and **political behaviour**. My research draws on statistical and econometric methods, spatial analysis, and new forms of social and geographic data to address empirical questions on these topics, with a focus on:
- The impacts of local economic, environmental, and policy shocks on people and places
- The formation of individual preferences within neighbourhoods and social networks
- The spatial dimensions of social processes

I completed my PhD Social Sciences and MSc in Data Science and Analytics at [Cardiff University](https://www.cardiff.ac.uk). My recently defended [PhD thesis](https://orca.cardiff.ac.uk/id/eprint/168198/) has been funded by the UK [Economic and Social Research Council](https://www.ukri.org/councils/esrc/), and examines the relationship between the geographic structure of social networks and electoral outcomes using aggregate social media data and large-scale administrative records. Prior to my doctoral studies, I held posts as a research officer at the UK Office for National Statistics and the Welsh Government.
